Core Subfields of Mathematics by Category

This page provides an overview of the fields of mathematics for study, but it is not completely exhaustive. Some of the more advanced fields of mathematics are, in fact, hard to fit neatly into one broad category.

Core Subfields of Math

Foundations and Logic

The study of the structure, and foundations of mathematical reasoning itself — what constitutes a rigorous proof, what is computable, and on which axiomatic systems mathematical theories can be built. It serves both as the starting point of mathematics and as a universal language that runs through all its branches.

Mathematical Logic

The study of formal systems, proofs, computability, and the foundational structure of mathematical reasoning.

Set Theory

The study of the properties of sets, infinities, ordinals, cardinals, and their axiomatic foundations.

Category Theory

A unifying abstract language describing mathematical structures and their relationships.

Model Theory

The study of the relationships between sentences in formal languages and the mathematical structures (models) in which they are true.

Analysis

The study of limits, continuity, change, and infinite processes in a rigorous sense.

Real Analysis

The rigorous study of real numbers, sequences in real, and limits, continuity, differentiation, and integration of real variable functions; the bedrock of analysis.

Complex Analysis

The rigorous study of complex numbers, sequences in real, and limits, continuity, differentiation, and integration of complex variable functions, with beautifully powerful integral theorems, series expansions, and residue theory.

Functional Analysis

The study of vector spaces endowed with some kind of limit-related structure and the linear functions defined on these spaces.

Measure Theory

A rigorous abstraction of length, area, and volume; the foundation of Lebesgue integration and probability theory.

Harmonic Analysis

The study of decomposing functions into superpositions of basic waves; an extensio of Fourier Analysis.

Calculus of Variations

The study of finding functions that extremize a functional (an integral of a function); closely linked to optimal control and minimal surfaces in geometry.

Geometric Analysis

The use of PDEs, calculus of variations, and functional analysis to study geometric and topological problems, such as minimal surfaces, Ricci flow, and spectral geometry.
